4. [ISDEEP] (Unconfined) Is the water table deep?

         DEPENDENCIES: AQ_TYPE
         
         LOGIC: 
         
         OUTPUT:
         ├─ A. (SHALLOW) (depth < 20m) → Continue to 5
         └─ B. (DEEP) (depth >= 20m) → Continue to 6

5. [K_SHALLOW] (Shallow) What is the shallow hydraulic conductivity (K)?
         
         INPUT: stratigraphy_table
         
         DEPENDENCIES: Shallow aquifer needs higher K to avoid
         water logging issues. 
         
         LOGIC: K for both saturated zone and vadose zone must be higher.
         Higher K in the saturated zone allows quick GW horizontal flow,
         and present water logging. 
         
         OUTPUT:
         ├─ K > 33 ft/day (High permeability)
         │  → (HIGHLY_FEASIBLE) → Continue to 16 (????)
         ├─ K = 3.3 - 33 ft/day (Moderate permeability)
         │  → (FEASIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS) (May require careful design)
         │  → Continue to 16
         └─ K < 3.3 ft/day (Low permeability)
            → (NOT_FEASIBLE) (Poor recharge capacity)

6. [LIMIT_LAYER] (Deep) Find limiting layer and K_min
Layer?
     
     INPUT: stratigraphy_table
     
     DEPENDENCIES: None
     
     LOGIC: in deep unconfined, we need to find the layer with minimum
     K in vadose zone. This layer will be used to define min_K and MAR storage Zone 
     
     OUTPUT:
     K_min, D_SZ_upper, limiting_layer_id
     
     Is the minimum K in the vadose zone < 0.3 ft/day?
         
         ├─ YES - Limiting Infiltration Layer Present
         │  → Continue to 2.1.3.1 (Mitigation Check)
         │
        └─ NO - Permeable Vadose Zone (K_min >= 0.3 ft/day)
            → (FEASIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS) (Surface or deep methods potentially
            viable) → Continue to 9 (Infiltration Rate Calculation)

7. [TOPSOIL] Is the limiting layer the TOP layer
AND < 3 ft thick?
         
         INPUT: limiting_layer_id, K_min
         
         DEPENDENCIES: LIMIT_LAYER
         
         LOGIC: if the first layer (top soil layer) is the limiting layer,
         and the thickness is small (< 3ft), this layer can be removed and
         surface spreading might be feasible
         
         OUTPUT:
         ├─ YES: (FEASIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS) (Earthwork/Removal Viable)
         │  (Surface spreading viable after low-K layer removal)
         │  → Continue to 9 (Infiltration Rate Calculation)
         └─ NO: (NOT_FEASIBLE) (Surface Spreading NOT FEASIBLE)            
            → Continue to 8 (Feasibility Follow-up)

8. [SHALLOW_LIMITING_LAYER]  Is limiting layer depth < 10 ft?       
         INPUT: limiting_layer_id, K_min
         
         DEPENDENCIES: TOPSOIL
         
         LOGIC: if limiting layer is relatively shallow, but not too deep
        (depth <= 10 ft), then trench spreading might be feasible.
         
         OUTPUT:
         ├─ YES: (FEASIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS) 
         │  (Trench and subsurface recharge methods are feasible)
         │  → Continue to 9 (Infiltration Rate Calculation)
         └─ NO: (NOT_FEASIBLE) (Trench Spreading NOT FEASIBLE)
              (Low-K layer is too thick or too deep for economic removal)
              (Deep injection likely required)            
            → Continue to 14 (follwo up on CONF_ACCESS)

9. [SPREAD_AREA] Calculate recharge area, infiltration rate, 
       available storage, gw_depth, 
     
     INPUT: (K_min), (max_available_area)
     
     DEPENDENCIES: LIMIT_LAYER
     
     LOGIC: 
    Using Hantush67 equation to find area that obtain infl_rate such that
    * infl_rate < k_min,
    * Operational Groundwater Depth (OpGW_depth) > D_min_GW, and
    * spread_area < max_available_area       
       
     OUTPUT:
     spread_area, OpGW_depth
     Is the calculated infiltration rate adequate for the intended recharge
     volume?
        ├─ YES: Continue to 10 (Spreading Area Estimation)
        └─ NO: (FEASIBILITY_DOUBTFUL) (Low infiltration capacity)
            → Continue to 10 (Spreading Area Estimation)
